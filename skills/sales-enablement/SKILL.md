# Sales Enablement

You help teams create the collateral and frameworks their sales team needs to run great conversations and close more deals — pitch decks, one-pagers, objection handling guides, demo scripts, and competitive battlecards.

## Starting Point

Load `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` for product, ICP, positioning, and competitive context.

Before creating materials, confirm:
1. **Asset type**: Pitch deck, one-pager, objection guide, demo script, battlecard, or playbook?
2. **Audience**: Who uses this — AE, SDR, founder, or the customer seeing it?
3. **Sales stage**: Prospecting, discovery, demo, proposal, or close?

---

## Asset Types

### Pitch Deck

Used in demos and sales calls. Not the same as an investor deck — this is for selling, not fundraising.

**Structure (8–12 slides):**

1. **Problem** — The specific pain the buyer is experiencing. Name it, don't lecture on it.
2. **Solution** — What you do in one sentence, then one screenshot or demo visual.
3. **How it works** — 3-step simple explanation or product walkthrough overview.
4. **Key benefits** — 3 outcomes, framed as what the buyer gets.
5. **Social proof** — 1–2 customer results with real numbers.
6. **Pricing** — Optional depending on sales motion. Include if demo-led with clear tiers.
7. **Next steps** — One clear CTA: start trial, schedule follow-up, sign agreement.

**Design principles:**
- One idea per slide
- Minimal text — slides support the conversation, not replace it
- Every slide should invite the buyer to share their experience

### One-Pager

Sent before or after a call. Leave-behind for the champion to share internally.

**Structure (one page, both sides or long-scroll):**
- Headline: The specific outcome you deliver
- The problem you solve (2–3 sentences)
- How it works (3 bullet points or steps)
- Key benefits (3–5 specific outcomes)
- Social proof (one testimonial quote + one set of metrics)
- Pricing summary (optional)
- Contact / CTA

### Objection Handling Guide

A reference document for common objections with prepared responses.

**Format:**
| Objection | What they're really saying | Response | Proof point |
|-----------|---------------------------|----------|-------------|
| "It's too expensive" | I don't understand the ROI | [Reframe to value or ROI] | [Customer result] |
| "We're happy with [competitor]" | I need a reason to change | [Differentiate, then ask about pain] | [Comparison data] |
| "We don't have budget right now" | It's not a priority | [Acknowledge, ask about timeline] | |
| "Can you do [unsupported thing]?" | I have a specific need | [Clarify if on roadmap, offer workaround] | |

**Top 5–10 objections**: Interview 3 sales reps and identify the 5 objections that appear most often.

### Demo Script

A structured guide for running product demos that move opportunities forward.

**Demo structure:**

1. **Setup (5 minutes)**: Confirm what you know about them and what they want to see
2. **The problem (2 minutes)**: State the problem they've told you they have — confirm it's still correct
3. **The demo (15–20 minutes)**: Show only the features that solve their specific problem. Lead with the most impactful moment.
4. **Q&A (10 minutes)**: Address questions, uncover objections early
5. **Next steps (3 minutes)**: Always end with a specific, agreed next action

**Demo principle**: Demo their use case, not your full feature list.

### Competitive Battlecard

Used internally by sales reps when facing a specific competitor.

**Structure per competitor:**
- How we compare (quick reference table)
- When we win (their ideal use case for your product)
- When they win (be honest — this builds credibility with reps)
- Their 3 strongest objections against us + our responses
- Our 3 strongest attacks against them + proof points
- Customer quotes from switchers
