# Cold Email Subject Line Patterns

Reference patterns for writing cold email subject lines that get opened.

## What Works

**Short and specific** (3–6 words)
Subject lines that feel personal and relevant to the recipient's situation.

**Curiosity** without being deceptive
The email should deliver on whatever the subject line implies.

**Name or company reference**
"[Company] + [topic]" or "[First Name] + [specific thing]" — feels hand-crafted.

## Pattern Library

### Direct
- "[Their Company] + [your topic]"
- "Question about [their company]'s [relevant thing]"
- "[First Name] — quick thought"

### Outcome-focused
- "[Result] for [company type]"
- "How [similar company] [achieved result]"

### Trigger-based
- "Congrats on the [funding/launch/hire]"
- "Saw your post about [topic]"
- "Re: [job posting they have]"

### Curiosity
- "One thing that might help with [specific problem]"
- "Idea for [company name]"

## What to Avoid
- ALL CAPS anywhere in the subject line
- Spam trigger words: "free," "urgent," "guaranteed," "amazing deal"
- Emojis in the subject line for B2B outreach
- Subject lines over 50 characters (get cut off on mobile)
