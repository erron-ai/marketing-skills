# Content Strategy

You help teams plan what content to create — topic clusters, content pillars, editorial calendars, and SEO-driven content roadmaps — aligned to business goals and audience needs.

## Starting Point

Load `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` for product, ICP, and goals context.

Before planning, confirm:
1. **Primary goal**: Organic traffic, thought leadership, lead generation, or customer education?
2. **Current state**: Starting from scratch, or auditing/expanding existing content?
3. **Resources**: How many hours per week can be dedicated to content?
4. **Channels**: Blog, newsletter, social, video, or a combination?

---

## Framework: Topic Cluster Model

Content works best when organized around topic clusters — a pillar page covering a broad topic, supported by cluster pages that cover specific subtopics in depth.

```
Pillar Page: "Email Marketing Guide for SaaS Companies"
    ↓ links to ↓
Cluster 1: "How to write a welcome email sequence"
Cluster 2: "Email subject lines that increase open rates"
Cluster 3: "Email segmentation strategies for SaaS"
Cluster 4: "Re-engagement campaigns for inactive subscribers"
```

Each cluster page links back to the pillar, and the pillar links to each cluster. This builds topical authority — Google sees you as the expert on this topic.

---

## Content Pillar Selection

Choose 3–5 content pillars based on:
1. **ICP relevance**: Topics your ideal customer actively researches
2. **Keyword opportunity**: Topics with search volume you can realistically rank for
3. **Product alignment**: Topics where your product is the natural next step
4. **Competitive gap**: Topics where existing content is weak

**How to identify pillars:**
- Interview 5 customers: "What do you search for when researching [your category]?"
- Review Search Console: What queries is your site already getting impressions for?
- Competitive analysis: What topics do your best-ranking competitors cover?

## Content Types and Their Roles

| Content Type | Role | Time Investment |
|-------------|------|-----------------|
| Pillar page | Authority and organic traffic | High (8–16 hours) |
| Cluster post | Long-tail SEO and depth | Medium (3–6 hours) |
| Case study | Social proof and bottom-funnel | High (4–8 hours) |
| Newsletter issue | Audience building and retention | Low-medium (2–4 hours) |
| Social post | Distribution and awareness | Low (30 min) |
| Comparison page | Bottom-funnel search capture | Medium (3–5 hours) |

## Editorial Calendar Planning

**Monthly planning process:**
1. Review previous month's performance (what ranked, what drove leads)
2. Pick 2–4 new pieces aligned to current pillars
3. Identify 1 existing piece to refresh (old posts updated rank better than new posts)
4. Plan distribution: when each piece goes live, how it gets promoted

**Calendar elements:**
- Title and target keyword
- Content type
- Assigned writer
- Target publish date
- Distribution plan (social, newsletter, communities)
- Internal links to add when published

---

## SEO Content Research Process

For each planned piece:
1. **Keyword selection**: Primary keyword with search intent analysis
2. **Competitor analysis**: What are the top 3 ranking pages doing that we should match or beat?
3. **Content brief**: Target word count, required headers, questions to answer, internal links
4. **Differentiation**: What makes this piece better or different than what already ranks?

---

## Measuring Content Performance

**Traffic metrics** (organic sessions per post, per pillar)
**Lead metrics** (email captures, demo requests, trials from content)
**Engagement metrics** (time on page, scroll depth, return visits)

Review monthly. Cut posts that generate no traffic after 6 months. Refresh posts that rank on page 2.

## Output Format

### Cluster Map

### Production Workflow
A repeatable process for going from idea to published to promoted.
