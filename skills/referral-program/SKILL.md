# Referral and Affiliate Program Design

You help teams design, launch, and optimize referral programs, affiliate programs, and word-of-mouth growth loops.

## Starting Point

Load `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` for product, pricing, and audience context. The reward structure must make sense given the economics of the product.

Before designing, confirm:
1. **Program type**: Customer referral (friend invites friend), affiliate (third-party earns commission), or both?
2. **Business model**: Subscription, one-time purchase, or usage-based?
3. **Customer LTV**: What is a customer worth on average?
4. **Current referral rate**: Are you getting any organic referrals already?

---

## Program Types

### Customer Referral Program

Users share your product with friends or colleagues. Both parties get a reward.

**When it works**: When the product has natural sharing moments (collaboration tools, consumer products, products that show in the output).

**Reward structures:**
| Type | Example | Best For |
|------|---------|----------|
| Bilateral discount | "Give 20%, get 20% off" | Subscription products |
| Credits | "Give $20, get $20 credit" | Usage-based products |
| Free time | "Give a month free, get a month free" | Trial-period products |
| Cash | "Earn $50 per referral" | High-LTV B2B products |

**Key design principle**: The referrer's reward should come after the referred user has experienced value — not immediately after signup. This filters for genuine referrals and reduces fraud.

### Affiliate Program

Content creators, agencies, and niche publishers earn a commission for sending paying customers.

**Commission structures:**
| Model | Typical Rate | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Revenue share (recurring) | 20–30% of MRR | SaaS with monthly subscriptions |
| One-time CPA | $50–200 per customer | Annual or one-time products |
| Tiered (performance-based) | Increases with volume | High-volume affiliates |

**Tools**: Rewardful and Tolt for Stripe-based SaaS. PartnerStack for larger programs.

## Referral Mechanics

### Finding the Right Moment to Ask

The best time to ask customers to refer is immediately after a success moment:
- Just activated (reached the aha moment)
- Just hit a usage milestone
- Just upgraded
- Just left a positive review or NPS response

Asking at random times or as part of onboarding (before value is proven) underperforms significantly.

### The Referral Message

Make it easy for the referrer to share with a pre-written message:
- Email template with their referral link pre-filled
- Twitter/LinkedIn share text ready to go
- A shareable asset (image, certificate, result) when appropriate

The less work the referrer has to do to share, the higher the participation rate.

## Optimization Levers

| Lever | How to Test |
|-------|-------------|
| Reward value | Higher reward vs. lower, cash vs. credit |
| Reward type | One-sided vs. bilateral rewards |
| Ask timing | After activation vs. after upgrade vs. after NPS |
| Message framing | "Share and earn" vs. "Help a friend and get rewarded" |
| Channel | In-app vs. email vs. post-payment confirmation |

---

## Anti-Fraud Measures

- Require the referred user to complete a paid action before reward is issued
- Set a minimum subscription period before the referrer earns the reward
- Limit rewards per referrer per period
- Monitor for self-referral patterns
