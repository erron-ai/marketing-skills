# Video Marketing

You help teams build a video content strategy — from YouTube channel architecture through video SEO, distribution workflows, and repurposing frameworks.

## Before You Start

Read `.agents/product-marketing-context.md`. Video marketing works differently across stages: early-stage companies should focus on problem-aware content; established brands can invest in brand storytelling and social proof.

## Video Content Types by Funnel Stage

| Stage | Goal | Video Type |
|-------|------|------------|
| Awareness | Introduce the problem | Educational, how-to, thought leadership |
| Consideration | Show the solution | Product demos, comparisons, case studies |
| Conversion | Reduce risk | Customer testimonials, ROI breakdowns, walkthroughs |
| Retention | Deliver ongoing value | Tips, advanced features, webinars |

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Build the YouTube Channel Architecture

Before creating videos, design the channel structure:

**Channel pillars**: 3–5 topic areas the channel will cover consistently
- Example for a marketing automation tool: "Email Strategy" / "Marketing Ops" / "Growth Tactics" / "Product Deep Dives"

**Playlists**: Group related videos so YouTube's algorithm can surface the series
- Each pillar gets its own playlist
- Create a "Start Here" playlist for new visitors

**Channel description**: Include primary keyword in the first 125 characters

**Channel trailer**: 60–90 second video that answers "Who is this for and what will you learn?"

### 2. YouTube SEO: Keyword Research

YouTube is the second-largest search engine. Treat each video as a keyword-targeted page.

For each video idea, research:
- **Search volume**: Use TubeBuddy, VidIQ, or Ahrefs' YouTube keyword tool
- **Competition**: How many strong videos already rank? Are the top results from large channels?
- **Intent**: Is the searcher looking to learn, compare, or buy?

Target keyword strategy:
- **Primary keyword**: Appears in title, first 25 words of description, 1–2 times in spoken transcript
- **Secondary keywords**: Mentioned naturally in description and tags
- **Long-tail opportunities**: Specific questions ("how to set up [Product] with Zapier") over generic topics

### 3. Video Production Framework

**Script structure for educational videos (the most common format)**:

```
[Hook — first 15 seconds]
State the problem or promise. Answer "Why should I keep watching?"
Example: "If your email open rates are below 25%, this one change will fix it."

[Introduction — 15–30 seconds]
Who you are, what the viewer will learn, why it matters now.

[Body — main content]
Divide into 3–5 clear sections. Use chapter titles (timestamps).
Each section: make a point → support with evidence → show the application.

[CTA — last 30–60 seconds]
One clear action: subscribe, comment, click the link in description, or try the product.
Never more than two CTAs.
```

**Thumbnail design principles**:
- One clear visual focal point (person, product, icon)
- Text under 6 words, readable at 120px wide
- High contrast colors; avoid stock photo backgrounds
- A/B test thumbnails after 1000 views (TubeBuddy)

### 4. Video Distribution Checklist

After publishing on YouTube, distribute to maximize reach:

- [ ] Embed in relevant blog posts within 24 hours
- [ ] Share on LinkedIn with a 3-sentence native text introduction
- [ ] Send to email list (or relevant segment) in the weekly newsletter
- [ ] Post a clip (30–60 sec) as a native upload to LinkedIn, Twitter/X, and Instagram
- [ ] Add to relevant onboarding or product sequences (if it's a tutorial)
- [ ] Pin as a comment with a useful timestamp guide

### 5. Repurposing Framework

One long-form video = multiple content assets. Map the transformation:

```
1 YouTube video (10–20 min)
  ↓
  3–5 short clips (60–90 sec) → LinkedIn, Instagram Reels, TikTok
  ↓
  1 blog post (expanded transcript + additional context)
  ↓
  1 LinkedIn carousel (key takeaways)
  ↓
  3–5 tweet/X threads (one insight per post)
  ↓
  1 email newsletter section
```

Use a consistent repurposing template: for each long-form video, create clips from moments that:
- Have a single, punchy insight
- Start with a hook in the first 3 seconds
- Can stand alone without the rest of the video

### 6. Video Analytics Framework

| Metric | Benchmark | What It Tells You |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Click-through rate (CTR) | 4–10% | Thumbnail + title appeal |
| Average view duration | > 40% | Content quality and pacing |
| Average views per viewer | > 1.5 | Channel relevance and loyalty |
| Subscriber conversion rate | > 0.5% | Long-term value signal |
| Traffic source: YouTube search | > 30% | SEO effectiveness |

Review monthly. Low CTR = fix thumbnail/title. Low view duration = fix pacing or hook.

### 7. Short-Form Video Strategy (LinkedIn, TikTok, Reels)

For short-form:
- **Hook**: First 3 words on screen determine if they swipe away
- **Format**: Talking head + B-roll OR screen recording with face cam
- **Length**: 45–90 seconds performs best on LinkedIn; 15–60 seconds on TikTok/Reels
- **Caption**: Always add subtitles (85% watch without sound)
- **Posting frequency**: 3–5x per week to test what resonates; pull back to 2–3x on winning formats
