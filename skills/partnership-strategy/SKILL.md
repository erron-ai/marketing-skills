# Partnership Strategy

You help teams design and execute partnership programs — from technology integrations and co-marketing arrangements to channel reseller programs and co-sell motions.

## Before You Start

Read `.agents/product-marketing-context.md`. Partnerships multiply what you already do well — they rarely fix a broken GTM. Make sure there's product-market fit and a working sales motion before investing in partnerships.

## Partnership Types

| Type | What It Is | When It Works |
|------|------------|--------------|
| Technology / Integration | Product integrates with another; listed in each other's marketplaces | Product is part of a workflow stack; integration creates stickiness |
| Co-marketing | Joint content, webinars, or campaigns with a partner's audience | Audiences overlap significantly; neither is a direct competitor |
| Referral / Affiliate | Partner earns commission for customer referrals | Clear attribution; partner's audience includes potential buyers |
| Channel / Reseller | Partner sells your product to their customer base | Enterprise sales requiring local presence or trusted advisors |
| OEM / White-label | Partner embeds your product in their own offering | Your product enhances theirs; both benefit from the bundling |
| Strategic / Alliances | Deep joint development or go-to-market commitment | Long-term shared market vision; complementary strengths |

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Define Partnership Goals

Choose the primary goal for each partnership type:
- **Distribution**: Reach new audiences you couldn't access alone
- **Revenue**: Generate pipeline or ARR through partner channels
- **Product**: Build integrations that increase stickiness and reduce churn
- **Credibility**: Associate with established brands to accelerate trust

Set 12-month targets for each:
- Number of active partners
- Partner-sourced pipeline ($)
- Partner-influenced revenue ($)
- New integration endpoints launched

### 2. Identify Partnership Candidates

For tech partnerships:
- Tools that share your ICP and appear frequently in customer tech stacks
- Platforms with marketplaces or app directories (Salesforce, HubSpot, Zapier, Slack)
- Tools your product integrates with or enhances

For co-marketing:
- Companies serving the same ICP without competing for the same budget
- Companies with larger audiences in your vertical
- Tools customers use immediately before or after yours in a workflow

Score each candidate:
| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Audience overlap with your ICP | 30% |
| Partner's brand credibility | 20% |
| Partner's willingness and capacity to partner | 20% |
| Revenue or pipeline potential | 20% |
| Ease of integration (for tech partnerships) | 10% |

### 3. Design the Partnership Structure

For each partnership, define:

**Value exchange**:
- What does the partner get? (Revenue share, co-marketing exposure, integration promotion, leads)
- What do you get? (Distribution, revenue, product stickiness, credibility)

**Responsibilities**:
- Who creates the content?
- Who manages the integration?
- Who handles joint customer support?

**Economics**:
- Referral fee: 10–20% of first-year ARR is standard for SaaS referral programs
- Revenue share for resellers: 20–40% margin depending on deal complexity
- Co-marketing: even split on production costs; each party promotes to their own audience

**Commitment levels**:
| Tier | Commitment | Benefits |
|------|------------|----------|
| Registered | List in directory, basic co-marketing | Logo on partners page |
| Select | Active referrals or integration | Co-marketing budget, training |
| Premier | Significant revenue contribution | Dedicated partner manager, joint pipeline reviews |

### 4. Build the Partner Enablement Kit

Partners sell better when they have the right materials:
- **Partner brief**: One-page overview of the product, ICP, and key messages
- **Positioning guide**: How to describe the partnership and product to their customers
- **Co-sell playbook**: How to identify joint opportunities and run a co-sell motion
- **Demo access**: Sandbox accounts and demo scripts
- **Sales training**: 30-minute recorded session on positioning, objections, and deal scenarios
- **Slack channel or portal**: Dedicated space for partner questions and deal registration

### 5. Launch and Activate the Partnership

Activation checklist:
- [ ] Integration or listing live in both products' ecosystems
- [ ] Both teams trained and briefed
- [ ] Co-marketing asset (blog post, webinar, case study) produced and published
- [ ] Partner listed on website partners page
- [ ] Partnership announced on social media (coordinated)
- [ ] First joint pipeline meeting scheduled

### 6. Run Co-Marketing Campaigns

Standard co-marketing plays:
- **Joint webinar**: Each company promotes to its own audience; share leads post-event
- **Co-authored blog post**: Published on one company's blog with backlink to the other
- **Joint email campaign**: Each company sends to their list with co-branded offer
- **Case study**: Highlight a mutual customer who uses both products together
- **Conference booth share**: Reduce costs by sharing exhibition space at industry events

### 7. Measure Partnership Performance

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Partner-sourced leads per quarter | [N] |
| Partner-sourced pipeline ($) | [$] |
| Partner-influenced revenue (%) | > 10% of total ARR |
| Active partners vs. registered | > 30% |
| Partner NPS (satisfaction survey) | > 50 |

Review quarterly. Remove inactive partners from the program after two quarters of no activity.

## Output

Document each partnership in `.agents/partnerships/[partner-slug].md` with the agreed structure, value exchange, active campaigns, and pipeline contribution. Maintain a master `.agents/partner-program.md` with program tiers, economics, and overall metrics.
