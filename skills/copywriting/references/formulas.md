# Copywriting Formulas and Frameworks

Reference formulas for common copy elements.

## Headline Formulas

### The Outcome Formula
"[Verb] [specific outcome] [without/in/for] [context]"
- "Generate 50 qualified leads per month without a dedicated sales team"
- "Close your books in 30 minutes, not 3 days"

### The Audience + Outcome Formula
"For [specific audience] who want [outcome]"
- "For SaaS founders who want predictable revenue without a sales team"

### The Before/After Formula
"Stop [painful current state]. Start [desired future state]."
- "Stop chasing data across five dashboards. Get every metric in one place."

### The Question Formula
"[Problem-aware question that the reader is already thinking]?"
- "Spending more time on reports than on the decisions they inform?"

## CTA Copy Patterns

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| Submit | Send My Results |
| Sign Up | Start My Free Trial |
| Learn More | See How It Works |
| Download | Get the Checklist |
| Get Started | Build My First Dashboard |

## Benefit Bullet Formula
"[Specific outcome] — [how it's achieved] — [why it matters]"
- "Ship reports in one click — not one afternoon — so your team spends time on insights, not data prep"

## Social Proof Patterns

### Metric proof
"[Company] [achieved measurable result] in [timeframe]"
- "Acme Corp cut reporting time by 70% in their first month"

### Role-specific testimonial
"As a [role], [specific thing it solved]"
- "As a solo founder, I was drowning in spreadsheets. Now I have the same visibility as a team of 10."
